Stage two

The bear

Picture it: you round a corner and a bear is on the path. Before you can even think, your heart slams and your body is ready to run. That isn't weakness — that's your amygdala firing in a fraction of a second to keep you alive.

Here's the catch: your brain can't always tell a real bear from an everyday worry. To the alarm, these can all be the bear.
