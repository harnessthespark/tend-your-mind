The container that holds the garden. You never tend it directly — but as you tend across sessions, the cracks acquire moss, ivy and wildflowers come through the gaps, and the light softens. Nothing is announced. This is pure web (SVG + CSS + JS) — no engine.

The mechanic: in the real build, integrity nudges up a hair each session you tend the garden (a bloom planted, a weed worked, the bucket filled) — never a slider, never a number the client sees. They just notice, three visits later, it looks a little more whole than it felt. One crack is left open-but-greened on purpose: vulnerability that became character, not damage that got sealed away. “Ancient and held-together, not damaged.”
