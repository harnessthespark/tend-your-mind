## 0. The spine

**You tend the conditions, not the thoughts.** Feelings are **weather** — they roll in, move through, and pass. You're the gardener and the sky, not the storm. The garden is the container; the **journey from a body-signal to a named, expressed feeling** is the spine inside it.

Audience: **AuDHD / alexithymia.** Design must obey four principles (Lisa):
1. **Simplicity** — clear, never overwhelming.
2. **Visual cues** — strong visuals compensate for verbal-emotional difficulty.
3. **Physical sensations** — bodily sensation is the bridge to emotion.
4. **Gradual complexity** — basic → nuanced, progressively.

## 1. Brain structures ↔ garden

| Structure | Role in emotion | Garden home |
|---|---|---|
| **Amygdala** | threat detection, salience | **the Alarm** (octagon — quiet when calm, rings when tripped) |
| **Hippocampus** | emotional memory + context | **the Potting Shed** (record of seasons) |
| **Prefrontal cortex** | regulation, decisions, impulse control | **the Gardener** (decides *what* to do; first to go quiet when overwhelmed) |
| **Hypothalamus** | autonomic + hormonal stress response | **Climate & Water** (the conditions; where regulation lives) |
| **Anterior insula** | **interoception — disrupted in alexithymia** | **the Sundial itself** (the instrument for reading the inner weather) / the gardener's *attunement* |
| **Anterior cingulate** | conflict monitoring, rumination | **the un-tipped bucket** (rumination) + **the head-heart crossroads** (Gardener↔Alarm conflict) |
| **Cerebellum** | timing, coordination, smoothing — **learned by repetition** | **the Gardener's hands / the green thumb** (how *deftly* and *well-timed* the tending is; grows with practice) |

**Two keystones for this audience:**
- **Insula = the sundial.** The whole body-first design is an **insula scaffold** — it does, externally and step-by-step, the interoception that runs faint in alexithymia. Worth saying out loud in-tool: *"this is the sense that's quiet in a lot of us; the dial lends it back to you."* Reframes the tool from *emotion picker* → *prosthetic for a faint sense*.
- **Cerebellum = why practice works.** It physically **smooths** a rehearsed skill. Every walk of the dial trains it — the read gets quicker, the response better-sized, more automatic. The neuroscience argument under the Layer-6 practice layer, and the garden's reason to *come back*.

## 2. The wheel — six weathers

A **"today's weather"** dial is weather only. The six: **anger · joy(enjoyment) · disgust · sadness · fear · surprise.** (Seeking & Care came off — they aren't weather; see §5.)

### Weather as a gradient (Atlas of Emotions)
Each emotion is a **range from mild → intense**, not one icon. The granular names are just **points on that range** — so "telling them apart" (Layer 4) and "weather" (Layer 5) are the **same axis: intensity.**

| Feeling | Weather | Mild → Intense |
|---|---|---|
| **Anger** | thunder & lightning | heat-haze simmer → full storm / lightning strike |
| **Fear** | **fog** | thin mist (unease) → thick fog (panic) — *the alarm gone haywire: shapes in the mist that may not be there, can't see what's real* |
| **Sadness** | rain | drizzle (melancholy) → downpour (grief) |
| **Enjoyment** | sun | sunny intervals (contentment) → blazing sun (happy) → rainbow (ecstasy) |
| **Disgust** | ⚠️ NEEDS LISA — *a sour, stagnant haze? a bad wind / smog?* (fog moved to fear) | — |
| **Surprise** | ⚠️ NEEDS LISA — *a sudden flash / a break in the cloud?* | — |

### Intensity = the radius
Show intensity **spatially**: near the **hub = mild**, out at the **rim = intense**. Tap the slice for *which* weather; reach out for *how much*. (This is the brief's "inner cores / outer granular" idea — now with a reason: the radius **is** the intensity.)

## 3. The autonomic axis — accelerator & brake

Under the "calm ↔ charged" coarse axis sits real physiology — your nervous system's **accelerator and brake**. The arousal numbers in the data = *which pedal is down*.

- **Sympathetic — accelerate** (mobilise: heart, breath, muscle)
- **Parasympathetic / ventral — settle** (calm, safe)
- **Parasympathetic / dorsal — shutdown / freeze** (numb, still, gone) ← *where this audience often lives*

**Three states, not two.** Shutdown explains the hardest distinctions: **depression isn't *more* sadness, it's sadness dropped into shutdown; freeze isn't *more* fear, it's fear in shutdown** — same accelerator emotion, brake slammed too hard. In AuDHD, shutdown is common and misread as "fine" or "lazy."

**Validates the spine:** *"calm the weather first, then the gardener gets back in"* = **applying the brake.** Tending the conditions *is* shifting accelerator → brake — metaphor and physiology are the same gesture.

Open: keep **three states** on rung one, or open with two (faster/slower) and surface "shutdown" only when someone lands in low-everything (simplicity rule)?

## 4. The guided walk (body-first)

For alexithymia: start from the **body you can notice**, not the **name you can't**. Same data, run backwards. Default in; **"I already know what I feel"** is a one-tap shortcut.

1. **Autonomic coarse** — speeding up · settled · shut down (+ pleasant/unpleasant). The easiest, grossest read.
2. **Where** — tap a **body figure** (visual, few). 
3. **What's it like** — show only the **handful of qualities still in play** for the candidates left standing (progressive reveal — never all at once).
4. **Narrow → pick** — the dial lights 1–2: *"this might be…"*; you choose.
5. **Tell apart** — the granular pair, foregrounded (*same racing body — anxiety or excitement?* = naming → understanding).
6. **Express** — *what's it asking for? how would you say it?* + the garden casts its weather.

Each rung adds **one** small thing; chip-count stays low throughout (principles 1 + 4). A quality only has to discriminate *within* a coarse quadrant (hot vs cold to split anger/fear), not across all six.

**Built so far:** keystone = steps 2–4 (where + what → narrow). **To do:** the autonomic coarse rung (step 1), tell-apart beat (5), express beat (6), progressive reveal.

## 5. Seeking & Care — not weather

Both are about **growth**, but they aren't weathers that pass — they're the **conditions of growth**.

- **Seeking** (dopamine; the always-on appetitive drive — curiosity, anticipation, the reach) = **the sun / the light** everything leans toward. The drive that *casts the shadow*. Not a slice — present in the whole instrument.
- **Care** (oxytocin; nurturing + bonding) = **the nurturing pour + the bond between gardens.** The watering can turns your *load* into *nourishment*; care especially flows **outward** — watering next door. The bucket itself stays **self-regulation** (your load/release); Care is what the water *becomes when it's given*.

Seeking = the *reach toward* the light. Care = the *water given* so something grows.

## 6. The SIGNATURE map (body → feeling)

Drives the body-first narrow. Body **regions** come from the bodily-emotion mapping work (Nummenmaa). **Qualities + valence** are the redline target. ⚠️ Clinical — Lisa's call.

| Feeling | Regions | Qualities (proposed redline) | Arousal | Valence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| anger | face · hands · chest | **hot** · tight · jittery | high (88) | −0.6 |
| fear | chest · gut · limbs | **cold** · racing · tight · breathless | high (85) | −0.7 |
| disgust | gut · throat · face | **queasy** · tight (throat) | moderate (55) | −0.7 |
| sadness | chest · throat · limbs | **heavy** · numb · lump-in-throat | low (25) | −0.7 |
| joy | whole body | **light** · buzzy/tingling · warm | high (90) | +0.9 |
| surprise | face · chest | **jolt/jumpy** · racing · breath-catch | very high (97) | 0 |

Redline notes: split overloaded `fluttery` → racing (fear) / buzzy (joy) / jolt (surprise); give surprise its own signature; joy = warm/light not anxious-flutter. **Coarse axes do most of the separating; the body is the tie-breaker (anger=hot vs fear=cold is the cleanest).**
